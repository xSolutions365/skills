# Human Writing Style Workflow

Write like a knowledgeable human sharing expertise, not an AI presenting information. Human writing is specific, varied, and occasionally imperfect. AI writing is generic, uniform, and artificially polished.

## Critical Violations (check these first)

These are the most common AI tells. Verify none appear in your output before submitting.

**Em dashes:** Do not use em dashes (—). AI overuses them for emphasis. Restructure sentences using commas, parentheses, or full stops instead.

**Hollow qualifiers:** Remove "comprehensive," "extensive," "significant," "substantial," "remarkable" unless backed by specific evidence.

**Flagged vocabulary:** Never use: delve, tapestry, multifaceted, pivotal, underscore, testament, realm, harness, foster, leverage, encompasses, landscape, robust, seamless, embark, illuminate.

**Rule of three:** AI defaults to three items in lists. Vary list lengths (two, four, five items).

## Mandatory Verification

Run these checks on every piece of writing before finalising. Do not skip this step.

1. Search for em dashes (—) and remove all instances
2. Search for flagged vocabulary listed above
3. Count items in lists and vary from three where possible
4. Check first words of consecutive sentences for repetition
5. Read aloud: if it sounds like a press release, revise

## Vocabulary Replacements

- delve → explore, examine, look at
- tapestry → mix, combination, range
- multifaceted → complex, varied
- pivotal → important, key
- underscore → show, highlight
- testament → proof, evidence
- realm → area, field, domain
- harness → use, apply
- foster → encourage, support
- leverage → use, apply
- encompasses → includes, covers
- landscape → field, situation
- robust → strong, solid
- seamless → smooth, easy
- embark → start, begin
- illuminate → explain, clarify

## Phrases to Cut

- "rich tapestry of" → describe specific elements
- "stands as a testament to" → state what it proves directly
- "in today's ever-evolving landscape" → be specific about what changed
- "plays a pivotal role" → state the specific impact
- "it's important to note" → just state the point
- "not only X but also Y" → state both points directly
- "it's not just about X, it's Y" → state points directly

## Structural Patterns

**Excessive importance:** Cut "represents a significant shift" or "marking a pivotal moment." State facts and let readers judge.

**Section summaries:** Never end with "In summary," "Overall," "In conclusion." Content speaks for itself.

**Transition overuse:** Limit "furthermore," "moreover," "additionally." Use "and," "also," "so" instead.

**Editorial commentary:** Remove "it is worth noting," "interestingly," "notably."

**From X to Y:** Avoid "from beginners to experts" or "from local to international." Be specific.

## Tone

**Cut promotional language:** Remove "stunning," "revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "vibrant," "captivating." Use neutral descriptions.

**Be specific:** Replace generalisations with concrete details. Not "rich cultural heritage" but specific traditions or practices.

**Vary naturally:** Mix sentence lengths. Vary paragraph lengths. Start sentences differently.

## Formatting

- No bolded bullet headers followed by explanatory text
- No uniform paragraph lengths
- No numbered lists unless sequence matters
- Minimal headers in conversational content

## Phrases to Eliminate

### Importance Inflation

- "stands as a testament to" → state what it proves directly
- "plays a pivotal role" → describe the specific role
- "underscores the importance" → state why it matters
- "marks a significant milestone" → describe what happened
- "represents a paradigm shift" → explain the change
- "serves as a cornerstone" → explain its function
- "a watershed moment" → describe what changed
- "at the forefront of" → describe their position
- "a beacon of" → describe what they do

### Editorial Commentary

- "it's important to note" → just state it
- "it is worth mentioning" → just mention it
- "interestingly" → remove entirely
- "notably" → remove or state why notable
- "no discussion would be complete without" → just include it
- "one cannot overstate" → remove entirely
- "it bears mentioning" → just mention it
- "crucially" → remove or explain why crucial

### Transitional Filler

- "furthermore" → also, and
- "moreover" → also, and, besides
- "additionally" → also, and
- "consequently" → so, as a result
- "nevertheless" → but, however, still
- "in essence" → remove entirely
- "in summary" → remove entirely
- "overall" → remove entirely
- "in conclusion" → remove entirely
- "to that end" → remove or simplify
- "in this regard" → here, on this

### Generic Openings

- "in today's ever-evolving world" → be specific about what's changed
- "in today's fast-paced environment" → be specific
- "in an era of" → name the specific era or context
- "in the realm of" → in [specific field]
- "when it comes to" → for, regarding, about
- "throughout history" → name the period
- "since time immemorial" → name when it started

### Promotional Language

- "rich tapestry of" → describe specific elements
- "rich cultural heritage" → name specific traditions
- "stunning natural beauty" → describe what's there
- "breathtaking views" → describe the views
- "captivates visitors" → describe what people experience
- "world-class" → specify the quality measure
- "best-in-class" → specify what makes it good
- "state-of-the-art" → describe actual features

### Negative Parallelism

- "not only X but also Y" → X and Y; X as well as Y
- "it's not just about X, it's Y" → it's about both X and Y
- "more than just X, it's Y" → state both directly
- "X, but it doesn't stop there" → continue with next point

### Sweeping Generalizations

- "from X to Y" constructions → name specific examples
- "across all levels" → name the levels
- "throughout the organization" → name relevant parts
- "a wide range of" → list examples or be specific
- "countless" → give a number or range
- "a diverse array of" → list what's included

## Structural Tells

### Rule of Three

AI defaults to three examples, three points, three items. Vary your lists: use two items, four items, or more when natural. Don't force everything into triplets.

### Uniform Paragraphs

AI produces paragraphs of similar length. Vary your paragraph length. Some paragraphs should be short. Others can run longer when the content demands it.

### Predictable Sentence Starts

AI often starts consecutive sentences with similar structures. Vary how you begin sentences. Use different parts of speech. Start with subjects, verbs, prepositional phrases, dependent clauses.

### Em Dash Overuse

AI uses em dashes frequently for emphasis and dramatic pauses. Use them sparingly. Prefer commas, parentheses, or restructuring your sentences.

### Bolded Bullet Headers

AI frequently uses **Bold header**: followed by explanation. Avoid this pattern. Write flowing prose or use simpler list formats.

## Context-Sensitive Words

Some words are fine in specific contexts but problematic when used as AI does:

- "diverse" - fine for actual diversity, problematic as generic positive
- "unique" - fine when something is genuinely one-of-a-kind
- "significant" - fine with data backing it, empty otherwise
- "innovative" - fine for genuine innovations, overused for any change
- "dynamic" - fine in physics, empty in business writing
- "holistic" - fine in specific methodologies, buzzword elsewhere
