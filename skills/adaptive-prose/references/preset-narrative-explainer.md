# Preset Guide: Narrative Explainer

## Objective

Define how to write prose that teaches or clarifies through a sustained argument rather than a pile of correct fragments.

## Required actions

1. Start with the reader's current situation or confusion point.
2. Build one continuous line of thought from premise to explanation to implication.
3. Prefer transitions, examples, and contrast over stacked bullet dumping.
4. Keep the language concrete even when the concept is abstract.
5. Keep it moderate in length: usually 350-800 words across four to seven paragraphs. Use a short list only when naming stages, contrasts, or examples makes the explanation clearer.
6. End in a place that leaves the reader better oriented, not merely informed.

## Done when

- The draft reads as one argument with visible progression.
- The explanation becomes clearer as it moves forward.
- The prose teaches without becoming fluffy or overlong.
- The layout supports the explanation instead of collapsing into same-shaped paragraphs.
