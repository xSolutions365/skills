# Preset Guide: Technical Deep Dive

## Objective

Define how to write precise, structured prose for technical readers who need reasoning, constraints, and implications.

## Required actions

1. State the conclusion or core claim early.
2. Surface assumptions, constraints, and tradeoffs explicitly.
3. Prefer exact mechanism language over summary adjectives.
4. Distinguish observed fact, inference, and recommendation.
5. Give the reasoning room to develop: usually 500-1200 words across five to nine paragraphs, with short flat lists only when separating mechanisms, failure modes, or decisions improves traceability.
6. End with implications, risks, or next engineering decisions.

## Done when

- The reasoning is traceable rather than implied.
- Technical precision is stronger than rhetorical polish.
- The reader can separate facts from judgments.
- The draft is visibly more developed and mechanism-heavy than an executive decision brief on the same material.
