# Preset Guide: Executive Decision Brief

## Objective

Define how to write decision-ready prose for executive or procurement-aware readers who care about implication, confidence, and next moves.

## Required actions

1. Open with the situation or decision pressure, not with process description.
2. State the recommendation or core takeaway early.
3. Connect every paragraph to consequence, tradeoff, risk, or action.
4. Use commercially concrete language instead of strategic filler.
5. Keep it concise enough to scan: usually 220-450 words, three to five short paragraphs, with a short flat list only when naming distinct decisions, risks, or next moves.
6. Prefer shorter sentences and one idea per paragraph so the reader can scan quickly.
7. Close with a next-step implication rather than a decorative summary.

## Done when

- The reader can name the situation, recommendation, and risk after one pass.
- The tone is direct, accountable, and commercially literate.
- The draft contains no brochure language or prestige filler.
- The output is materially tighter than a technical deep dive on the same source would be.
