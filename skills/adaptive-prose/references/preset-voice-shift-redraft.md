# Preset Guide: Voice-Shift Redraft

## Objective

Define how to revoice an existing piece when the facts are usable but the tone, framing, or structure is too weak for the audience.

## Required actions

1. Preserve factual content, scope, and evidentiary limits from the source.
2. Recompose the structure when a stronger argument or cadence is needed.
3. Shift tone toward the selected audience without adding unsupported confidence.
4. Remove generic phrasing, borrowed prestige language, and weak transitions.
5. Reset length and layout to fit the new audience rather than the original cadence, but avoid unnecessary expansion; if the rewrite grows, the added space must buy clearer audience fit.
6. Run both factual parity and commitment parity before accepting the rewrite.

## Done when

- The rewrite sounds materially stronger than the source.
- The new voice fits the target audience.
- The rewrite has not invented stronger claims than the source can support.
- The audience shift is obvious in both tone and compression level.
