# Preset Guide: Operator Brief

## Objective

Define how to write short, high-signal prose for execution-focused readers who need the answer quickly.

## Required actions

1. Lead with the answer, decision, or status.
2. Keep supporting detail to what changes the reader's action.
3. Use short transitions and concrete nouns.
4. Remove scene-setting, generic reassurance, and rhetorical flourish.
5. Keep the brief compact: usually 80-220 words, one to three short paragraphs, with a short flat list only when there are distinct actions or blockers.
6. End with the next action, implication, or blocker when one exists.

## Done when

- The first lines carry the core answer.
- The reader does not need to hunt for the point.
- Every sentence earns its place.
- It is visibly shorter and sharper than any adjacent preset would be.
