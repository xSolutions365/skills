# Redraft Integrity Guide

## Objective

Keep redrafts stronger than the source without letting them drift from what the source actually says.

## Required actions

1. Decide whether the job is tightening, restructuring, or revoicing before rewriting.
2. Preserve factual content unless the brief explicitly authorizes factual correction.
3. Preserve commitment level. Do not turn cautious source language into stronger claims.
4. Preserve scope. Do not add promises, guarantees, or broader applicability not present in the source.
5. Where the source is ambiguous, clarify only to the degree supported by the source or by supplied context.
6. After rewriting, compare the new draft to the source for:
   - factual parity,
   - commitment parity,
   - audience fit,
   - net improvement in clarity.

## Done when

- The rewrite is clearer than the source.
- Facts, scope, and commitment remain aligned with the source.
- Any structural change is justified by reader comprehension.
